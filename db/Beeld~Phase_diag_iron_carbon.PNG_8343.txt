   Iron-carbon phase diagram under atmospheric pressure

   This diagram is limited by pure iron on the left and by iron carbide on
   the right. The mains phases are:
     * α iron: ferrite, ferritic steel
     * γ iron: austenite, austenitic steel
     * iron carbide: cementite, Fe[3]C.

   We can see an eutectic and an eutectoid; these phases crystallise as a
   stacking of fine strips of pure phases (iron and cementite) in case of
   the eutectoid, or a pure iron containing small balls of cementite for
   the eutectic. Although it is heterogeneous, these phases behave like
   homogeneous pure bodies.

   Steel is between 0 and 2 mass percent of carbon. Cast iron is between
   1,7 and 6,67%.
