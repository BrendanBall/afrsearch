[edit] Summary

   This diagram shows height to tip of the tallest structural elements in
   each building. If measured from the ground to the highest attached
   component, the Sears Tower is the tallest in the world, but as the
   antennas are not actually structural; they aren't considered part of
   the building, and the Sears Tower building itself is shorter than
   Taipei 101 and the Petronas Towers. Using the method of ranking heights
   which counts decorative spires but not antennas, the tallest towers go
   in order from Taipei 101, Petronas Towers, and then the Sears Tower
   followed by the Jin Mao Tower. Sears tower has the most floors at 110,
   and has a higher roof than the roof of the Petronas Towers.
