Wikipedia:Robot


          For bot operators that want to run their bots, please see the
          appropriate section below.
     __________________________________________________________________

   'n Robot (in die konteks van Wikipedia) is 'n program wat kontak met
   Wikipedia het oor die web. Dit verwys dus nie na die programme wat op
   die bedieners van die Wikimediastigting loop nie. Robotte word
   byvoorbeeld gebruik om interwiki-skakels in stand te hou, aangesien
   hierdie proses maklik geoutomatiseer kan word.

   Mense is egter vir meeste take beter as robotte en die gebruik van
   robotte moet beperk word om te verseker dat sulke tegnologie slegs
   gebruik word tot die verbetering van Wikipedia. As 'n robot byvoorbeeld
   gebruik word om 'n klomp artikels outomaties te vertaal, sal dit bloot
   beteken dat daar klomp swak vertaalde artikels bygekom het.

   Dit is belangrik om te besef dat die beleid vir die gebruik van 'n
   robot heelwat verskil tussen verskillende tale van Wikipedia. Dra altyd
   sorg om eers die beleid van 'n spesifieke projek uit te vind en dit te
   volg. Omdat 'n outomatiese proses wysigings baie vinnig kan aanbring,
   word beleid gespesifiseer vir die beskerming van die projek. Die
   blokkering van robotte en die terugrol van robotte se wysigings kan
   baie makliker geskied aangesien 'n veronderstelde negatiewe impak
   moontlik baie groter kan wees.

[wysig] Hoe werk dit?

   'n Sekere program kan 'n gebruiker in staat stel om een of ander
   wysiging op die wiki uit te voer. Die proses kan grootliks outomaties
   wees, of elke wysiging kan deur 'n mens nagegaan word. 'n Robot gebruik
   'n gewone rekening, net soos 'n mens, en die wysigings verskyn by die
   onlangse wysigings en in elke betrokke bladsy se geskiedenis. Vir
   robotte wat baie wysigings maak en wat algemeen vertrou word om hierdie
   wysigings sonder kontrole te maak, kan aansoek gedoen word vir
   amptelike robotstatus. Dit beteken dat die wysigings nie meer by die
   onlangse wysigings sal verskyn tensy mens spesifiek klik op "wys
   robotte". Die wysigings van 'n robot met amptelike robotstatus sal
   steeds in elke betrokke bladsy se geskiedenis verskyn.

[wysig] Applications for robot status

   (In Engels ter wille van besoekende robotoperateurs wat nie Afrikaans
   magtig is nie.)

   Please realise that we, the Afrikaans speaking human editors of the
   Afrikaans Wikipedia, have limited resources, and really want to write a
   good, Free encyclopedia in Afrikaans. Time used to evaluate robot
   applications, robot edits, or any other aspect of robot life, takes
   away our time that could be spent on writing, editing, translation,
   public relations, etc. Therefore please make sure that you can clearly
   indicate the advantage your robot brings to the Afrikaans Wikipedia
   project. If you think we really want the most up to date interwiki
   links to empty date articles in other languages of Wikipedia, you are
   mistaken, and really need to re-evaluate things from our perspective.

   Your robot should at least comply with these requirements:
     * a user page for your robot, explaining:
          + the robot's page should explain what the robot does - the
            purpose and usefulness of the robot for the Afrikaans
            Wikipedia project
          + the software used by the robot, including the version number
            or your policy for updating from the version control system
          + who is responsible for the robot
          + whether its activities are attended or not - please be as
            specific as possible
          + whether it is permanent or temporary. If temporary, mention
            the time that it will be operating in.
     * Mention your application in the talk page of this article.

   Please realise that your application may take a very long time (some
   volunteers need to review your application, and interested volunteers
   might not be available/willing all the time). Please also realise that
   the local bureaucrats can only give bot status to robots who's
   applications have been reviewed by a locally trusted human. Please
   understand that local administrators are encouraged to block robots if
   any problem is suspected - there is no need to take it personally.
   Similarly, not all administrators might feel inclined to search for
   your preferred talk page through lots of redirects onto Wikipedia
   projects with user interfaces in languages that they don't understand.
   Human time is more precious than computer (robot) time.

   Robots without a robot flag are allowed to edit, but only if their
   edits are infrequent (a few per hour). This is necessary to enable
   review of your activities before granting robot status, or might be
   required if the nature of your robot's work actually requires human
   review.
